Back in January, blogTO featured a creative Ontario Airbnb that caught everyone's attention. The property offered guests rooms themed after iconic movies and TV shows, turning a regular rental into something guests would actually remember.

Look, we see a lot of cookie-cutter rentals. Beige walls, generic furniture, the same IKEA prints everyone has. But this property took a completely different approach that's worth talking about.

## What Made This Property Stand Out

The Airbnb featured rooms designed around popular films and television series. Each room had its own distinct personality and immersive experience. Guests weren't just booking a place to sleep — they were booking an experience.

And honestly? That's what separates good hosts from great ones in today's market.

This type of theming works because it gives people something to talk about. Your guests post photos, tell their friends, maybe even book again just to try a different room. It's marketing that does itself.

## The Business Case for Creative Theming

Here's the thing about themed properties — they solve two major problems most Ontario hosts face right now.

**Problem one:** Standing out in search results. When someone scrolls through dozens of identical listings, something unique catches their eye. A movie-themed room gets clicked before another "cozy downtown space."

**Problem two:** Justifying higher rates. Generic rentals compete on price. Unique experiences compete on value. Guests will pay more for something they can't get anywhere else.

The numbers back this up too. Properties with strong themes and unique selling points consistently outperform standard rentals in both occupancy rates and average daily rates.

## What Ontario Hosts Can Learn

You don't need to go full Hollywood to apply these lessons. Small creative touches can make a big difference:

Start with what you know. Love hockey? Create a Toronto Maple Leafs den. Into cottage life? Go all-in on the Muskoka aesthetic. The key is authenticity.

Focus on one room first. You don't need to theme your entire property overnight. Pick your best bedroom or common area and make it special.

Think about your local market. What draws people to your area? In Niagara, wine country themes work. Near Algonquin Park, outdoor adventure vibes make sense.

But remember — execution matters more than the concept. A poorly done theme is worse than no theme at all.

## The Practical Side of Themed Properties

Managing a creative property does come with extra considerations. Themed decor might need more maintenance. Specific items could be harder to replace if damaged.

You'll also want to be thoughtful about your target audience. A horror movie theme might not work for family-friendly areas. Know your market before you commit to major changes.

And here's something most hosts miss — document everything well in your listing photos. If you've created something special, make sure potential guests can see it clearly in your photos and description.

## Making It Work for Your Property

Start small and test the waters. Maybe it's vintage movie posters in one room or a TV series collection in your entertainment area. See how guests respond before making bigger investments.

Pay attention to your reviews too. Guests will tell you what resonates. If everyone's talking about your Star Wars bathroom decor, you know you're onto something.

The goal isn't to create a museum — it's to create an experience that feels intentional and memorable.

Ontario's short-term rental market keeps getting more competitive. Properties that find ways to stand out and create genuine experiences will always have an advantage over those that don't.

Whether you're running a single property or managing multiple rentals, there's room to add personality and create something guests actually want to book again.
