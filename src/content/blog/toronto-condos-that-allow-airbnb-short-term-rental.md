Wondering if your Toronto condo allows Airbnb? We analyzed **every short-term rental registration** filed with the City of Toronto and ranked all 435 condo buildings by how many units are actively registered.

The numbers tell the story: **3,001 condo units** are registered for short-term rentals across **435 buildings**. Seven buildings have over 100 registrations each. If your building is on this list, at least one owner has successfully operated an Airbnb there.

## Key Stats

- **7,422** total STR registrations in Toronto (all property types)
- **3,001** condo unit registrations
- **435** unique condo buildings with at least one STR
- **7** buildings with 100+ registrations
- **48** buildings with 10+ registrations
- **Spadina-Fort York** ward has 61% of all condo STRs (1,830 registrations)

## Before You Start

Having a building on this list does not guarantee you can Airbnb your unit. You still need to:

1. **Register with the City of Toronto** (your principal residence only)
2. **Check your condo corporation's bylaws** (the condo board can prohibit STRs even if the city allows them)
3. **Respect the 180 night annual cap** for entire-home rentals (no limit for partial-unit, where you rent rooms while home)
4. **Display your registration number** on all listings
