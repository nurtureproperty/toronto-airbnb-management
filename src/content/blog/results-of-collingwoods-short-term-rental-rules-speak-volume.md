Collingwood Mayor Yvonne Hamlin says the town's short-term rental regulations are working better than expected. The results, she claims, "speak volumes" about the effectiveness of their approach to managing vacation rentals in this popular Blue Mountain resort area.

So what's actually happening on the ground? And what can hosts in Collingwood learn from these early results?

## The Numbers Don't Lie

Here's the thing about Collingwood's STR rules. They were designed to balance tourism revenue with neighbourhood concerns, and the data suggests they're hitting that mark.

Mayor Hamlin points to several positive indicators since the regulations took effect. The town is seeing better compliance from property owners. Fewer noise complaints from residential areas. And tourism dollars are still flowing into the local economy.

But the most telling result? More hosts are actually engaging with the licensing process instead of operating under the radar.

## What Collingwood Got Right

The town's approach focused on clear rules rather than outright bans. They created defined zones where short-term rentals can operate. Set reasonable licensing fees. And established enforcement mechanisms that actually work.

This isn't the heavy-handed approach some Ontario municipalities have taken. Instead, Collingwood built a system that acknowledges vacation rentals are part of their tourism economy while addressing legitimate community concerns.

The mayor's confidence in these results suggests other resort towns might follow a similar path.

## Why This Matters for Collingwood Hosts

If you're running a [short-term rental in the Collingwood area](/services/short-term-rental-management-toronto), these positive results are great news. They signal the town is committed to working with hosts rather than against them.

But it also means compliance isn't optional anymore. The success of these regulations depends on host participation in the licensing system.

Here's what you need to stay on top of:

**Licensing requirements** - Make sure you're properly registered with the town. The streamlined process is working, but only if you use it.

**Zoning compliance** - Double-check your property is in an approved area for short-term rentals. The defined zones are clear, but it's your responsibility to verify.

**Community standards** - The reduction in complaints means hosts are being better neighbours. Keep that momentum going with proper guest management.

## Looking Ahead

The mayor's positive assessment suggests Collingwood's regulations are here to stay. That's actually good news for serious hosts who want long-term stability in their rental business.

Other Blue Mountain area municipalities are watching these results closely. If you operate rentals in nearby towns, expect similar regulations to roll out based on Collingwood's success.

The key is getting ahead of these changes instead of reacting to them.

## Making Compliance Work for You

Compliant hosts get more community support. Towns get better tourism revenue. Guests get higher quality experiences. It's not a perfect system, but it's working better than anyone expected.
