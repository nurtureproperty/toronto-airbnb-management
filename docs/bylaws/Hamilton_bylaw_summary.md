# Hamilton Short-Term Rental By-law Summary

**By-law Number:** 07-170 (Schedule 32)
**Title:** By-law to License and Regulate Various Businesses - Short-Term Rentals
**Added to By-law:** By-law 23-005, January 25, 2023
**Last Consolidated:** November 2023
**Municipality:** City of Hamilton, Ontario

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Short-Term Rental** | All or part of a dwelling unit for sleeping accommodations for less than 28 consecutive days in exchange for payment |
| **Principal Residence** | Dwelling unit owned/rented where individual ordinarily resides, makes home, and conducts daily affairs |
| **Operator** | Individual who operates a STR (cannot be a corporation) |
| **Short-Term Rental Broker** | Person who advertises/facilitates STR reservations via internet and receives payment/benefit |
| **Reservation** | Booking commitment between Operator and guest for specified time period |

---

## Principal Residence Requirement

- **Required:** Yes - STRs must be the Operator's Principal Residence
- **Proof Required:** Government-issued ID demonstrating STR is principal residence
- **One License Limit:** No more than one STR Operator license per person
- **Corporations:** STR Operator licenses cannot be issued to corporations

---

## Licensing Requirements

### Operator License

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Eligibility** | Individual 18+ years (no corporations) |
| **License Fee** | As per User Fees and Charges By-law |
| **Validity** | 1 year (annual renewal) |
| **Limit** | One license per Operator |

### Application Requirements

1. **Personal Information:**
   - Operator name, phone, email
   - Address of STR property
   - Emergency contact (available 24/7)

2. **Property Documentation:**
   - Floor plan with square footage and bedrooms
   - Smoke alarm and CO detector locations/dates
   - Description of parts used for STR
   - Building type description
   - Number of off-street parking spaces

3. **Safety & Compliance:**
   - Detailed fire escape plan
   - Proof of insurance ($1M liability minimum)
   - Condo board/owner approval (if applicable)
   - Statutory declaration of Fire Code compliance
   - Zoning verification for secondary dwelling units

4. **Background Check:**
   - Original criminal record (or police certification of no record)
   - List of criminal/provincial offences (convicted, not pardoned)

5. **Operational Requirements:**
   - Guest information package
   - List of advertising platforms used
   - Government ID proving principal residence

## Insurance Requirements

### Operator Insurance

| Coverage Type | Minimum Amount |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Liability** | $1,000,000 per occurrence |
| **Coverage For** | Personal injury, bodily injury, death, property damage |
| **Cancellation Notice** | 30 days written notice to City |

**Must Include:**
- Short-term rental or home sharing activity coverage
- Homeowners, condo, or renters insurance as appropriate

### Broker Insurance

| Coverage Type | Minimum Amount |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Commercial General Liability** | $2,000,000 per occurrence |
| **Comprehensive Crime Insurance** | $50,000 minimum |
| **Cyber Liability & Privacy Breach** | $1,000,000 per occurrence |

---

## Occupancy Limits

| Limit Type | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| **Per Sleeping Room** | Maximum 2 persons |
| **Children Exemption** | Children 2 years and under not counted |
| **Advertising** | Must display maximum guest limit |

---

## Operating Requirements

### Advertising
- Display Operator's license number prominently
- Display maximum overnight guest limit
- Remove prohibited ads within 7 days of notice
- Only use licensed Brokers

### Fire Safety
- Post fire escape plan on all floors
- Working smoke alarms per Fire Code
- Working carbon monoxide detectors per Fire Code
- Floor plans showing safety equipment locations

### Guest Information Package
Must be provided electronically at booking AND in print in unit:
- Contact info for Operator/designate (24/7 availability)
- 9-1-1 emergency instructions
- Two nearest hospitals/emergency services
- Hamilton Police non-emergency contact
- Floor plan with evacuation routes and safety equipment
- Notice of any recording devices
- Waste disposal instructions (collection day, recycling)
- Parking instructions
- Copy of Operator license with occupancy limits

### Record Keeping
Maintain for 3 years:
- Number of nights rented
- Nightly and total price charged
- Any other info required by Director

---

## Short-Term Rental Broker Requirements

### License Requirements
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Fee** | As per User Fees and Charges By-law |
| **Validity** | 5 years |
| **Insurance** | $2M CGL + $50K Crime + $1M Cyber |

### Broker Obligations

1. **Data Agreement:** Execute agreement with City for data collection/disclosure
2. **Consent:** Obtain Operator/guest consent for info disclosure
3. **Record Keeping (3 years):**
   - Listing ID and license numbers
   - Total nights rented per listing per year
   - Revenue collected per listing per year
   - Complaints received per listing
4. **Reporting:**
   - Submit records to Director quarterly
   - Make specific address records available within 7 days
   - Report removed listings to City
5. **Complaints:**
   - Maintain public procedure for handling complaints
   - Have system for receiving/responding to public complaints
6. **Communications:**
   - Convey municipal communications to Operators as prescribed

---

## Prohibited Activities

Operators shall NOT:
- Operate without a license
- Operate at non-principal residence
- Exceed guest limits
- Fail to display license number in ads
- Use unlicensed Brokers
- Provide false information
- Let insurance lapse
- Fail to remove prohibited ads within 7 days

---

## Enforcement

### Inspections
Director may audit/examine:
- All books and records
- Accounts, vouchers, documents
- Electronic documents
- Any records related to STR business

### Compliance Requirements
Upon audit direction, must:
- Give reasonable assistance
- Answer questions (orally or in writing)
- Attend premises or City office
- Produce documents on oath if required

### License Refusal/Revocation Grounds
- Past conduct suggesting non-compliance
- Application by corporation (for Operators)
- Outstanding orders (Property Standards, Building Code, Fire Code, Health)
- Zoning non-compliance
- Outstanding fines/penalties/taxes
- Criminal conviction within past 5 years
- False/misleading information
- Adverse to public interest or health/safety

---

## Penalties

- Fines as per Provincial Offences Act
- Continuing offences provisions apply
- Maximum fines up to $100,000 for corporations
- Premises closure orders possible up to 2 years

---

## Appeals Process

**Appeal Body:** Hamilton Licensing Tribunal

**Tribunal Composition:**
- Minimum 3 Council members
- Selected as Chair from members
- Secretary assigned for administrative duties

**Process:**
- Submit appeal within time prescribed
- Written grounds required
- Hearings conducted per Statutory Powers Procedure Act
- Tribunal may confirm, modify, or rescind Director's decision
