# Ottawa Short-Term Rental By-law Summary

**By-law Number:** 2021-104
**Also Known As:** Short-Term Rental By-law
**Effective Date:** June 1, 2021 (or when By-law 2021-106 came into effect)
**Last Updated:** January 24, 2024 (includes amendments 2021-311, 2023-519, 2024-27, 2024-469)

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Short-Term Rental** | Transient accommodation in whole or part of a residential unit for less than 30 consecutive nights, marketed via a platform |
| **Principal Residence** | Residential unit owned/rented where person ordinarily resides, conducts daily affairs, and has no other property designated as such |
| **Cottage Rental** | STR that is NOT the principal residence, located in authorized zones under Zoning By-law Section 121B |
| **Dedicated Short-Term Rental** | Pre-existing hotel use that operates as legally non-conforming |
| **Host** | Person who operates or provides a short-term rental |
| **Property Manager** | Agent who manages advertising, bookings, guest services for hosts |
| **Short-Term Rental Platform** | Service that markets/brokers STR bookings via website or app |

---

## Principal Residence Requirement

- **Required:** Yes - STRs must be your principal residence
- **Exceptions:**
  - Cottage rentals (in designated rural zones under Section 121B)
  - Dedicated Short-Term Rentals (pre-existing hotel uses)
- **Limit:** One STR permit per principal residence + one cottage rental permit (if applicable)

## Licensing Requirements

### Host Permit

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Permit Fee** | $65 administration + $58 permit fee = **$123 total** |
| **Validity** | 2 years |
| **Replacement Copy** | $20 |
| **Renewal** | Apply within 30 days prior to expiry |

**Application Requirements:**
- Completed application with name, address, contact info
- Proof applicant is 18+ years of age
- If renting: written landlord consent
- Proof of principal residence (unless cottage/dedicated STR)
- Floor plan with square footage and number of bedrooms
- Proof of insurance ($1M liability)
- Signed declaration of by-law compliance
- No unpaid fines or user fees with City

### Property Manager Registration

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Fee** | $65 administration + $156 registration = **$221 total** |
| **Validity** | 1 year (annual renewal) |
| **Insurance** | $2M Commercial General Liability |

**Additional Requirements:**
- Criminal Records and Judicial Matters Check (within 90 days)
- Business address in Ottawa (not P.O. box)
- Declaration of no outstanding criminal charges

### Platform Registration

| Tier | Listings | Fee |
|------|----------|-----|
| **Tier 1** | Less than 100 | $65 admin + $1,084 = **$1,149** |
| **Tier 2** | 101-500 | $65 admin + $2,711 = **$2,776** |
| **Tier 3** | More than 500 | $65 admin + $5,421 = **$5,486** |

**Validity:** 3 years (trial period)

## Insurance Requirements

| Category | Coverage Required |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Hosts** | $1,000,000 per occurrence (homeowners/condo/renters insurance with STR coverage) |
| **Property Managers** | $2,000,000 Commercial General Liability |
| **Platforms** | $2,000,000 Commercial General Liability |

**All policies must:**
- Include 30 days written notice to City before cancellation
- Be satisfactory to City Solicitor
- Coverage lapses invalidate permits/registrations

## Occupancy Limits

| Unit Type | Max Sleeping Rooms | Max Overnight Guests |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Dwelling Unit / Mobile Home** | 4 rooms | 8 guests |
| **Oversize Dwelling Unit** | 8 rooms | 10 guests |
| **Legally Non-conforming Dwelling** | 8 rooms | 10 guests |

**General Rule:** Maximum 2 persons per sleeping room

**Note:** Director has discretion to reduce limits for public interest, health/safety, or nuisance concerns.

---

## Operating Requirements

### Advertising
- Must display host permit serial number prominently
- Must display maximum overnight guest limit
- Remove prohibited listings within 72 hours of notice

### Guest Information Package
Must be provided electronically at booking AND in print in the unit:
- Host/property manager contact info
- 9-1-1 emergency instructions
- Nearest 2 hospitals/emergency services
- 3-1-1 and ottawa.ca access instructions
- Non-emergency police contact
- Floor plan with evacuation routes and safety equipment
- Notice of any video/audio recording systems
- Waste disposal instructions
- Parking instructions
- Smoking/vaping rules
- Common offences and fines schedule
- Copy of host permit with serial number and conditions

### Fire Safety
- Functioning smoke alarms (as per Ontario Fire Code)
- Carbon monoxide alarms (if fireplaces/fuel-burning appliances present)
- ABC class fire extinguisher on each floor, accessible to guests

---

## Prohibited STR Locations

Short-term rentals are NOT permitted in:
- Accessory buildings (detached garages, sheds)
- Motor vehicles or trailers
- Unlawful residential units
- Community housing
- Non-principal residence properties (except cottage rentals/dedicated STRs)
- Buildings where landlord/condo/co-op has registered prohibition

---

## Platform Requirements

Platforms must:
- Register with the City
- Maintain records for 3+ years:
  - Municipal address of each listing
  - Total nights rented per calendar year
  - Total revenue per listing per year
  - MAT collected
  - Complaints received
- Submit records to Director quarterly
- Provide specific address records within 48 hours upon request
- Have complaint handling system
- Collect and remit Municipal Accommodation Tax (4%)
- Convey municipal communications to hosts

---

## Enforcement

### Inspections
By-law Officers may:
- Enter property at reasonable times (non-residential portions)
- Require production of documents
- Make examinations, take samples/photos
- Audit records of hosts, property managers, and platforms

### Suspension
- Director may suspend without notice for by-law violations
- Reinstatement possible once violation corrected

### Revocation
Director may revoke permit/registration for:
- Non-compliance with by-law
- Technical/clerical error in issuance
- False/misleading information provided
- Operation adverse to public interest
- Health/safety risks
- Significant public nuisance

---

## Penalties

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| **Minimum fine** | Up to $500 |
| **Maximum fine** | Up to $100,000 per day |
| **Continuing offences** | No $100,000 cap on total |
| **Special fine** | May exceed $100,000 |

**Obstruction of by-law officer:** Separate offence

---

## Appeals Process

**Appeal Body:** Property Standards and License Appeals Committee

**Timeline:**
- File written request within 14 days of Director's decision
- Hearing scheduled at least 14 days from receipt of request

**Applicant Rights:**
- May be represented by counsel
- Can adduce evidence and cross-examine witnesses
- Onus on applicant to show cause

**Committee Powers:**
- May uphold, modify, or reverse Director's decision
- May impose conditions
- May suspend for less than unexpired permit period
- Decision is final and binding

---

## Landlord/Condo/Co-op Prohibitions

**Building owners, condo corporations, and housing co-ops may:**
- Register a prohibition against STRs with the Director
- Fee: $65 administration + $5 per residential unit

**Effect of prohibition:**
- No host permits issued for affected units
- Existing permits revoked

---

## Municipal Accommodation Tax (MAT)

- **Rate:** 4%
- **Collection:** Platforms must collect from guests
- **Remittance:** Quarterly to City's Financial Services
- **Reporting:** Monthly to Chief Financial Officer

## Quick Reference for Hosts

1. STR must be your principal residence (unless cottage rental exception applies)
2. Obtain host permit ($123 for 2 years)
3. Maintain $1M liability insurance
4. Display permit number on all listings
5. Provide guest information package
6. Maximum 2 guests per bedroom, 8-10 total depending on unit size
7. Install proper fire safety equipment
8. Keep records for 3 years
9. Renew permit within 30 days before expiry
