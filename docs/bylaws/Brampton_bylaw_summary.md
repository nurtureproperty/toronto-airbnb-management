# Brampton Short-Term Rental By-law Summary

**By-law Number:** 165-2021
**Title:** Short-Term Rental By-law
**Effective Date:** July 8, 2021
**Municipality:** City of Brampton, Ontario

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Short-Term Rental (STR)** | All or part of a Principal Residence for sleeping accommodations for less than 28 consecutive days in exchange for payment |
| **Principal Residence** | Dwelling unit owned or rented by a person where they ordinarily reside |
| **Short-Term Rental Host** | Individual person who owns, operates or offers a Principal Residence for STR |
| **Short-Term Rental Guest** | Person(s) responsible for the rental via rental agreement |
| **Entire-Unit Rental** | STR where guest occupies entire dwelling unit |
| **Bedroom** | Room for rent/hire used for sleeping accommodations, may contain bathroom |

---

## Principal Residence Requirement

- **Required:** YES - must be the Host's Principal Residence
- **Limit:** Host deemed to have only ONE Principal Residence at any time
- **Prohibition:** Cannot rent/advertise unless it's your Principal Residence at that time
- **Auto-Invalidation:** License automatically invalidated if property ownership or principal residency changes

---

## Key Restrictions

| Restriction | Limit |
|-------------|-------|
| **Maximum Nights/Year** | 180 nights per calendar year |
| **Maximum Bedrooms** | 3 bedrooms rented individually at one time |
| **Minimum Host Age** | 18 years |
| **Corporations** | Cannot be Host (individuals only) |

---

## Occupancy Limits (Based on City's Minimum Maintenance By-law)

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Base Occupancy** | 1 person per 14 sq. metres (150 sq. ft.) of habitable rooms |
| **Children under 12** | Count as 0.5 persons |
| **Minimum Room Width** | 1.83 metres (6 feet) |
| **Minimum Room Area** | 5.6 sq. metres (60 sq. feet) |
| **Two+ Person Room** | 3.7 sq. metres (40 sq. feet) per person |
| **Ceiling Height** | 2.3m over 50% of floor area OR 2.13m average |
| **Prohibited Sleeping** | Kitchen, bathroom, hallway |

## Licensing Requirements

### License Fees (Schedule A)

| Fee Type | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| **Application or Renewal Fee** | $150.00 |
| **Late Renewal Fee** | $50.00 |
| **Closed File Administration** | $50.00 |

*Fees auto-increase annually by Toronto CPI*

### License Term
- Expires September 30th annually
- Must obtain/renew by September 30th each year

### Application Requirements

1. **Property Documentation:**
   - Address with declaration it's Principal Residence
   - Floor plan showing STR areas, square footage
   - Evidence of property ownership OR tenant consent from owner
   - All fees paid

2. **Insurance:**
   - $2,000,000 Commercial General Liability
   - Per occurrence for personal injury, bodily injury, death, property damage
   - Certificate of Insurance in prescribed form
   - 10 days notice of cancellation to City

3. **Criminal Record Check:**
   - Conducted by Police in applicant's jurisdiction
   - If corporation/partnership: check all officers, directors, partners
   - No outstanding charges/warrants
   - No criminal convictions within last 3 years

4. **Guest Information Package** (must be provided to all guests):
   - Host name and 24/7 emergency contact phone
   - Floor plan with safety equipment and exits
   - Police emergency and non-emergency contacts
   - Health emergency contacts (nearest emergency services)
   - Waste disposal and property maintenance info
   - Lawful parking instructions
   - Complaint procedures
   - Fee schedule (if applicable)
   - Approved occupancy limit and fire safety plan

5. **Declaration:** Compliance with by-law and accuracy of information

## Operating Requirements

### Record Keeping
Must keep for **6 years** following last day of rental:
- Number of nights rented
- Nightly and total price charged
- Whether Entire-Unit or Partial-Unit Rental
- Any other info required by Licence Issuer

Provide records within 30 days of request.

### Advertising
- Include license number on ALL documents (invoices, contracts, receipts)
- Include license number in ALL advertisements
- Cannot advertise property that's not properly licensed

### Posting Requirements
- Post license in conspicuous place clearly visible to renters

### Host Liability
- Host liable for conduct of all guests
- Responsible for ensuring guests comply with all laws including municipal by-laws

### Notifications
- Notify Licence Issuer within 7 days of any changes to application info
- Notify immediately of any by-law violations

## Grounds for License Refusal/Revocation

License may be refused or revoked if:

1. Contrary to public interest (health, safety, nuisance, consumer protection)
2. Reasonable grounds Host won't operate with integrity/honesty
3. Would infringe rights of public members
4. Issued on false, mistaken, or incorrect information
5. Business in prohibited area
6. Business not carried on for 60+ continuous days
7. Applicant indebted to City (fines, taxes, penalties)
8. Building doesn't comply with by-laws, Building Code, Fire Code
9. Premises in unsafe/dangerous condition
10. Carrying on will result in by-law breach
11. License fee unpaid
12. Additional fees remain unpaid
13. **3 or more convictions** under this by-law within one year

### Automatic Ineligibility
Cannot hold license if in past 12 months:
- Applied and was finally refused
- Had license finally revoked

## Enforcement

### Inspections
Licence Issuer or Inspector may enter at reasonable times to verify:
- By-law compliance
- Order compliance
- License conditions
- Orders under Municipal Act s.431

**Inspection Powers:**
- Require production of documents, records, books
- Inspect and remove documents for copying
- Require information from any person
- Make examinations, take tests/samples/photos

### Orders to Comply
May issue orders to:
- Discontinue contravening activity
- Correct contravention
- Both discontinue and correct

### Return of License
- Upon revocation/suspension, immediately return license
- Inspector may enter premises to take/remove license

## Penalties

| Offence Type | Fine Range |
|--------------|------------|
| **Minimum Fine** | $500 |
| **Maximum Fine** | $100,000 |
| **Continuing Offence** | $500 - $10,000 per day (no $100,000 cap on total) |
| **Multiple Offence** | $500 - $10,000 per offence (no $100,000 cap on total) |
| **Special Fine** | May exceed $100,000 (for economic advantage/gain) |

Administrative Penalties also apply under By-law 218-2019.

## Appeals Process

**Appeal Body:** Brampton Appeal Tribunal

**Timeline:**
- File written appeal within 15 days of receiving notice of decision
- Pay applicable appeal fee (per User Fee By-law)

**Filing:** Filed with Clerk along with reasons in support

**Appeal Process:**
- Does NOT suspend Licence Issuer's decision
- Tribunal hearing scheduled with reasonable notice
- Tribunal decision is final
- If appeal not filed within 15 days, decision is final

**Tribunal Powers:**
- May order license granted, reinstated, suspension ended
- May change conditions
- Applicant must still fulfill outstanding license requirements

## 4% Municipal Accommodation Tax (MAT)

Brampton applies a 4% MAT on STR bookings.

## Discrimination Prohibited

No Host shall discriminate based on:
- Race, ancestry, place of origin, colour, ethnic origin
- Citizenship, creed, sex, sexual orientation
- Gender identity, gender expression
- Age, marital status, family status, disability

**Service Animals:** Must permit persons with service animals on licensed premises.
