# St. Catharines Short-Term Rental By-law Summary

**By-law Number:** 2023-OGO
**Title:** Short-term Rental Accommodation Licensing By-law
**Effective Date:** 2023
**Replaces:** By-law No. 2021-67
**Municipality:** City of St. Catharines, Ontario

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Short-term Rental** | Home-based business, entire dwelling unit rental for max 28 consecutive days |
| **Bed and Breakfast** | Home-based business, guest room(s) rented for max 28 days, meal provided, owner present/available |
| **Principal Residence** | Dwelling unit where person resides majority of year (183+ days) |
| **Home-based Business** | Use within dwelling unit secondary to primary residential use |
| **Long-term Tenant** | Tenant renting for at least 12 months |
| **Agent** | Person appointed by Licensee to ensure compliance |

---

## Principal Residence Requirement

- **Required:** YES - must be Principal Residence of Owner OR Long-term Tenant
- **Definition:** Reside 183+ days per year
- **Long-term Tenant:** Must have 12+ month lease AND owner's written consent

---

## License Types

| Type | Description | Owner On-Site |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Short-term Rental** | Entire dwelling unit | Not required |
| **Bed and Breakfast** | Guest room(s) only | Must be present/available |

## Licensing Requirements

### License Term and Fees
| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **License Period** | 2 years from issuance |
| **Fees** | Per City's Rates and Fees By-law |
| **Refundable** | Generally NO (Director's discretion) |

### License Expiry Events
- Automatic expiry upon:
  - Change in ownership (for Owners)
  - Termination/expiry of lease (for Long-term Tenants)

### Application Requirements

1. **Applicant Eligibility:**
   - Must be Owner or Long-term Tenant
   - Must be 18+ years of age
   - If Long-term Tenant: written owner authorization

2. **Property Documentation:**
   - Municipal address and legal description
   - Transfer/deed (Owner) OR lease + owner consent (Tenant)
   - Site sketch showing: STR location, adjacent Highway, garbage/recycling facilities
   - Floor plan: room locations, dimensions, photos, proposed use, bed count
   - Parking Management Plan (Zoning compliant)
   - Fire Safety Protocol

3. **Safety Compliance:**
   - Licensed electrician certificate (within 12 months) - Electrical Safety Code compliance
   - Fire Safety Protocol approved by Fire Chief

4. **Insurance:**
   - $2,000,000 minimum per occurrence
   - Types: "home sharing", "host insurance", "short-term rental", "B&B" insurance
   - Property damage and bodily injury coverage
   - City as additional insured (if requested)

5. **Declarations:**
   - Sworn statement of accuracy
   - Consent for City to verify information
   - Consent to inspection
   - Attestation of Principal Residence status

## Insurance Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Minimum Coverage** | $2,000,000 per occurrence |
| **Coverage Type** | General liability with property damage and bodily injury |
| **Acceptable Types** | Home sharing, host insurance, short-term rental, B&B |
| **Additional Insured** | City (if requested) |
| **Annual Update** | Within 3 months of every license anniversary |

## Operating Requirements

### License Display
- Post legible copy within 1 metre of interior primary entrance
- When premises occupied for STR/B&B purposes

### Fire Safety Protocol Display
- Post legible copy within 1 metre of interior primary entrance
- Include evacuation routes, safety equipment locations

### Advertising
- Include license number in ALL advertisements and promotions

### Agent/Property Manager
- Must be available to attend within **1 hour** of contact (phone or email)
- Contact info must be provided to City

### Change Notifications
- Notify Director in writing within **7 days** of any changes to application info

### Parking
- Vehicles parked ONLY in spaces identified on Parking Management Plan
- Must comply with Zoning By-law

## UNIQUE: Demerit Point System

St. Catharines uses a points-based enforcement system.

### Point Thresholds

| Total Points | Consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| **5 points** | 3-month license suspension |
| **10 points** | License revoked for remaining duration |
| **15 points** | License revoked - NO appeal right (unless leave granted) |

### Points Duration
- Points remain for **2 years** from date allocated

## License Renewal

### Application Requirements
- Submit complete renewal application
- Declaration if no changes to floor plan, site sketch, Parking Plan, Fire Protocol
- Revised documents if any changes made
- New electrician certificate if electrical changes made
- Prescribed fees

### Deadline
- Submit at least **3 months before expiration** or may require new application
- Renewal application does NOT permit continued operation until approved

---

## Grounds for Non-Issuance/Revocation

Director may refuse or revoke license if:

1. Past conduct suggests non-compliance with law or lack of integrity
2. Issuance adverse to public interest
3. Poses threat to health and safety
4. Applicant owes fines/fees to City
5. Subject to orders under:
   - City by-laws
   - Building Code Act
   - Fire Protection and Prevention Act
   - Medical Officer of Health
6. Non-compliance with Zoning By-law
7. Objection from Chief Building Official or Fire Chief
8. Previous license suspended/revoked
9. License revoked/suspended within last 2 years

---

## Appeals Process

**Appeal Body:** Hearing Officer (appointed by Council)

### For Non-Issuance/Non-Renewal
- Request hearing within **10 days** of decision notice
- Hearing within **30 days** of notice

### For Suspension/Revocation
- Request hearing within **30 days** of notice
- Hearing within **60 days** of notice

### Appeal Requirements
- Submit appeal form
- Pay applicable appeal fee
- Submit supporting documents via email before hearing
- No new material at hearing

### Hearing Process
- Video/audio enabled (electronic hearings permitted)
- Only permitted attendees (Hearing Officer, Applicant/Licensee, Director, staff)
- Absence = deemed abandoned appeal

### Hearing Officer Powers
- Affirm, vary, or overturn Director's decision
- Make any decision Director was entitled to make
- Decision is **final**

### Automatic Suspension
For public health/safety issues:
- May suspend automatically (max 14 days unless extended)
- Must provide reasons (verbal or written)
- Opportunity to respond before revocation

---

## Enforcement

### Inspections
- At reasonable times when permitted by law
- In accordance with Powers of Entry By-law
- May assist with any person necessary

### Obstruction
- Cannot hinder or obstruct Director, Officer, or City employee
- Cannot hinder work to bring STR into compliance

### Presumption
- If advertisement found, Owner/Long-term Tenant deemed to have posted or consented

### Advertising Without License
- Cannot advertise, promote, broker, or offer STR without valid license number

---

## Penalties

- Per Municipal Act, 2001
- Administrative penalties under Non-Parking AMPS By-law
- Continuing offence: each day/part of day is separate offence

## 4% Municipal Accommodation Tax (MAT)

St. Catharines applies a 4% MAT on STR bookings.

## Quick Reference for St. Catharines Hosts

1. **Principal Residence required** (183+ days/year)
2. **2-year license**
3. **$2M insurance** required
4. **Licensed electrician certificate** required (within 12 months)
5. **Fire Safety Protocol** required
6. **Parking Management Plan** required
7. **Post license** within 1m of entrance
8. **Include license number** in all ads
9. **Agent/contact available within 1 hour**
10. **Demerit system:**
    - 5 points = 3-month suspension
    - 10 points = revocation
    - 15 points = revocation with no appeal
11. **Points last 2 years**
12. **Renew 3 months before expiry**
